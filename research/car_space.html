Transport space in GB

This small website includes interactive tables from [Miner et al. 2026 paper]. Results are based on data from Ordnance Survey and OpenStreetMap.

Transport-allocated land is presented at three geographic levels:

  • Cities: Urban centres based on the degree of urbanisation classification and a minimum population of 100,000.
  • Local authorities: In Scotland and Wales, there are 32 and 22 local authorities, respectively. In England, the local authority structure is more complex; this table displays "lower tier local authorities (LTLAs)".
  • Postcodes: Postcode districts are the "first half" of a postcode, such as "EH8" for part of central Edinburgh.

Across all geographies, "HH" stands for "households." Land areas are given in square kilometres (km2). Population density is in people per km2 of buildings (building footprints). Transport land areas are given in square metres (m2) per person.
